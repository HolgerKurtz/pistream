How it works
Track IDs & trails Each moving blob is assigned a number. That number follows the bird until it disappears for too many frames — then it's dropped. The coloured line is its recent path; older positions are dimmer. IDs reset to 0 when you pause tracking. Motion threshold How much a pixel must change from the background before it counts as movement. Lower = more sensitive — picks up subtle flickers, but also wind and lighting changes. Raise it to ignore small variations and only track clearly moving objects. Blob size (px²) The video is 640 × 480 px. Size is measured in square pixels:
  • Insect / speck of dust: ~5–15 px²
  • Small bird (sparrow, swallow): ~20–150 px²
  • Large bird (gull, heron): ~150–600 px²
  • Aeroplane / cloud edge: 1000+ px²
Watch Blobs this frame above the sliders — it shows the live range of what the tracker is seeing, so you can dial min/max to match. Min track age A blob must appear for this many consecutive frames before it's drawn. Increase it to filter out one-frame flashes (rain drops, lens flare). Decrease it if real birds are disappearing too quickly. Calibrating background On startup the tracker spends a few seconds learning what the static sky looks like. Nothing is detected during this window — that's normal.
