How it works ▸ expand
How birds are detected The tracker runs at maximum sensitivity — any moving pixel is a candidate. The only filter that matters is brightness: birds are dark against the sky, clouds are bright. Sky brightness is measured automatically on startup, on resume, and every 15 seconds to adapt as clouds move through the frame. Bird colour Slide left to detect lighter-coloured birds (more sensitive, but drifting clouds may also register). Slide right to only track obviously dark silhouettes against the sky. The default "light / medium" position works well for most conditions. Track IDs & trails Each moving blob gets a persistent number. The coloured trail is its recent path; older positions are dimmer and thinner. IDs reset to 0 when you pause tracking. Trail length How many past positions are drawn. The label shows frames and approximate seconds at the current frame rate. Longer trails reveal more of the flight arc; shorter trails reduce clutter when many birds cross at once. Trail thickness Maximum line width at the tip (newest point). The trail tapers to 1 px at the tail so direction of travel is always clear. 1–2 px is subtle; 4–8 px is bold. Calibrating background On startup the tracker spends a few seconds learning the static sky. Nothing is tracked during this window — that's normal.
